Women's Reservation in Panchayati Raj

Constitutional provisions ensuring women's participation in grassroots democracy

73rd Constitutional Amendment Act, 1992

The 73rd Amendment to the Constitution mandated reservation of seats for women in Panchayati Raj Institutions, ensuring their participation in local governance.

Reservation Structure

•

33% Reservation: One-third of total seats reserved for women at all three levels of Panchayati Raj.

•

Chairperson Positions: One-third of chairperson positions reserved for women in rotation.

•

SC/ST Women: Additional reservation for women belonging to Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes.

Three-Tier System

•

Gram Panchayat (Village Level): Direct election of women representatives at village level with reserved seats and chairperson positions.

•

Panchayat Samiti (Block Level): Intermediate level with women's representation in block-level governance and development planning.

•

Zilla Panchayat (District Level): District-level governance with women's participation in policy formulation and resource allocation.

Powers and Functions

Women representatives in Panchayati Raj institutions have authority over 29 subjects listed in the 11th Schedule, including agriculture, health, education, and rural development programs.

Impact of Women's Participation

•

Policy Focus: Increased attention to women and child welfare, health, and education issues.

•

Resource Allocation: Better allocation of resources for community needs, especially water, sanitation, and healthcare.

•

Transparency: Enhanced transparency and accountability in local governance and fund utilization.

•

Social Change: Breaking traditional barriers and empowering women to participate in decision-making processes.

Challenges and Solutions

•

Capacity Building: Training programs for women representatives to enhance their leadership and administrative skills.

•

Financial Literacy: Programs to improve understanding of budgets, financial planning, and resource management.

•

Social Support: Creating supportive environments to overcome cultural and social barriers to women's participation.
