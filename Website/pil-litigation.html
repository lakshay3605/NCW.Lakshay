Public Interest Litigation (PIL)

Legal tool for citizens to seek justice for public interest and disadvantaged groups

What is PIL?

Public Interest Litigation allows any citizen to approach the court for the protection of public interest and the rights of disadvantaged groups. It enables access to justice for those who cannot approach courts themselves.

  • Protects rights of marginalized communities
  • Addresses issues of public concern
  • Ensures government accountability
  • Promotes social justice

How to File a PIL

Step-by-step guide on filing a Public Interest Litigation for women's rights and social justice issues.

  • Identify a genuine public interest issue
  • Gather relevant facts and evidence
  • Draft the petition clearly
  • File in appropriate High Court or Supreme Court

Filing Procedure

Detailed procedure and requirements for filing a writ petition or PIL in the appropriate court.

  • Prepare petition with proper format
  • Attach supporting documents
  • Pay required court fees
  • Submit to court registry

Required Documents

Essential documents and information needed for filing a PIL petition.

  • Petition in prescribed format
  • Affidavit supporting facts
  • Relevant evidence and documents
  • Court fee payment receipt
