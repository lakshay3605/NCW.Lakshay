Article 21 - Right to Life and Personal Liberty

The most fundamental right protecting life and liberty

Constitutional Text

"No person shall be deprived of his life or personal liberty except according to procedure established by law."

Key Provisions

  • Right to Life: Fundamental right to live with dignity
  • Personal Liberty: Freedom from arbitrary detention and interference
  • Due Process: Protection through established legal procedures
  • Broad Interpretation: Includes various aspects of dignified living

Expanded Scope for Women

The Supreme Court has interpreted Article 21 broadly to include several rights crucial for women:

  • Right to Privacy: Protection of personal autonomy and bodily integrity
  • Right to Health: Access to healthcare, including reproductive health
  • Right to Education: Access to quality education without discrimination
  • Right to Livelihood: Opportunity to earn a living with dignity
  • Right against Sexual Harassment: Safe working and living environment

Women-Specific Applications

  • Protection against domestic violence
  • Right to reproductive choices
  • Safe motherhood and maternal health
  • Protection from trafficking and forced labor
  • Right to shelter and basic amenities

Landmark Judgments

Several Supreme Court cases have used Article 21 to protect women's rights, including cases on sexual harassment at workplace, right to abortion, and protection from domestic violence.

Contemporary Relevance

Article 21 continues to evolve as a powerful tool for protecting women's rights in modern India, addressing new challenges and ensuring comprehensive protection of women's life and liberty.
