Article 14 - Equality before Law

Constitutional guarantee of equal treatment under the law

Constitutional Text

"The State shall not deny to any person equality before the law or the equal protection of the laws within the territory of India."

Key Provisions

  • Equality before Law: No person is above the law, and everyone is subject to the same legal system
  • Equal Protection: Similar treatment in similar circumstances
  • Non-discrimination: Protection against arbitrary state action
  • Universal Application: Applies to all persons, including women, within Indian territory

Significance for Women

Article 14 ensures that women receive equal treatment under the law and are protected from discriminatory practices by the state. This fundamental right forms the foundation for gender equality in India.

Judicial Interpretations

The Supreme Court has consistently held that Article 14 prohibits class legislation but permits reasonable classification. This has been crucial in upholding women-specific laws and affirmative action measures.
