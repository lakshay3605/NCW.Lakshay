Article 15 - Prohibition of Discrimination

Protection against discrimination on various grounds including sex

Constitutional Text

"The State shall not discriminate against any citizen on grounds only of religion, race, caste, sex, place of birth or any of them."

Key Provisions

  • Non-discrimination: Prohibits discrimination based on sex, among other grounds
  • Public Access: Equal access to public places, shops, restaurants, and wells
  • State Action: Applies specifically to state discrimination
  • Reasonable Classification: Allows special provisions for women and children

Special Provisions for Women

Article 15(3) empowers the State to make special provisions for women and children, enabling affirmative action and protective legislation specifically designed for women's welfare and advancement.

Impact on Women's Rights

  • Prohibition of sex-based discrimination in public spaces
  • Legal foundation for women's reservation policies
  • Protection against gender-based exclusion from public facilities
  • Basis for women-specific welfare schemes

Landmark Cases

The Supreme Court has used Article 15 to strike down discriminatory practices and uphold women's rights to equal treatment in various spheres of public life.
